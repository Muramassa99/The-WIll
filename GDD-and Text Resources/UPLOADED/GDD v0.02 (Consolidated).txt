1) HIGH CONCEPT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
A hub-based, PvE-first, instanced “99-floor” dungeon expedition game with:
- 3D skillshot combat (including melee), dodging, blocking, CC windows.
- No hard classes: equipment + crafted weapon/gear defines playstyle.
- Deep crafting via micro-block design: shape + materials create unique items and behavior.
- Seasons reshuffle difficulty: each floor is a NAMED layout; its floor NUMBER changes (difficulty + drop mods).
- Social hub + contracts board to form parties, trade, and commission crafting.

Player fantasy:
- “I craft something personal, learn the place when it’s safe, then return when it’s deadly.”
- “People matter, knowledge matters, creation matters.”

Core pillars:
1) People matter (party coordination, contracts, reputation, crafting identity)
2) Knowledge matters (map discovery, enemy patterns, floor mastery)
3) Creation matters (crafted gear defines gameplay; provenance; templates)
4) Seasons create memory (difficulty rotations change focus without wiping knowledge)

Design stance:
- Early development focuses on mechanics, movement, and systems (cubes/primitives).
- Art polish comes later as a “skin” over a proven core.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
2) CORE LOOP
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Gather → Craft → Attempt Floor → Extract/Return → Refine Build → Climb Difficulty

- Enter a floor instance from the hub.
- Explore (personal fog-of-war), gather materials, fight mobs/bosses.
- Return to hub; process materials; craft/upgrade gear; adjust build.
- Re-run the same NAMED floors at higher difficulty (new season / new rotation).
- Chase clears, titles, prestige, and better materials.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
3) WORLD STRUCTURE & INSTANCING
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Hub/Town:
- Prototype hub is minimal: 4x4 platform + station placeholders.
- Stations planned: entry portal, crafting/forge access, storage, vendor, cooking, repair.
- Social: chat, mail, contracts board, party forming, inspection.

Floors (99):
- Each floor is a NAMED layout (e.g., “Grace’s Garden”) with a stable map/layout identity.
- Seasonal rotation remaps NAMED floors to FLOOR NUMBERS (difficulty + drop quality).
- Layout remains constant; difficulty and drop quality shift with floor number.
- Higher difficulty may add extra enemies/events/mechanics (optional later).

Instance model:
- Client loads only the current scene (hub OR one floor OR instanced crafting zone).
- Server-side instancing is planned later (authoritative outcomes, economy integrity).

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
4) EXPLORATION & MAP DISCOVERY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Fog-of-war:
- Map reveal is PERSONAL and permanent once discovered for a NAMED floor.
- Party members are always visible on the map as dot + name (even in fog).
- Teleporting into unknown reveals a local “island bubble” around landing point.

Party-size reveal bonus (low impact):
- Reveal radius is centered on YOUR character.
- If party members are physically within your reveal radius, you gain a small bonus (cap ~+20%).
- Intended as a mild co-op quality-of-life, not a balance pillar.

Design intent:
- Easy difficulty versions emphasize discovery/navigation.
- Hard difficulty versions emphasize combat mastery (not “being lost while in danger”).

Landmarks.
-Locations that allow extraction can must be atoned to be used. (comes with risks)

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
5) COMBAT OVERVIEW (PVE FIRST)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Core combat:
- 3D OTS camera with zoom option.
- All attacks are skillshots (including melee).
- Hits require being inside the attack volume; misses are real.
- I-frames exist only on dedicated skills (usually mobility type).

Damage philosophy (PvE baseline):
- Enemy NPC damage is “flat damage.” No damage flavor needed for the core PvE model.
- Defense is a flat stat (“Armor” / “Defense”). Damage vs Defense reduces HP (and/or "Bubble shields").
- (PvP is optional later; PvP zones may apply a global damage-vs-player reduction modifier.)

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
6) COMBAT RESOURCES (HP / BUBBLE-SHIELDS / ARMOUR & ENDURANCE / STAMINA / COOLDOWNS )
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Resource model:
- HP + Armor & Endurance + Stamina + cooldown-based skills (no mana/energy resource).

HP:
- Primary survivability pool (base HP + gear + buffs).
- Displayed as numeric value + bar.

Armor
 - This is the sum of all defense stats listed on armor pieces, earrings, and other gear

Endurance 
 - This is the core stat, representing a character's basic ability to defend. It directly multiplies the defense value provided by armor. Higher endurance means greater damage reduction. tipically found on belts and armor pieces as sub stats 

Bubble-Shield (Temporary HP):
- Bubble-Shield is a flat-value temporary HP pool added on top of HP (not % based).
- Bubble-Shield is visually displayed as an extension/overlay on the HP bar (distinct color).
- Bubble-Shield is primarily granted by House of Prayer effects and select support skills.
- Bubble-Shields can be abundant (can reach ~2x HP or more depending on build).
- Bubble-Shield mitigation uses reduced defensive effectiveness:
  - Bubble uses 50% of the defender’s effective Defense in mitigation calculations.
  - Balancing knob: BUBBLE_SHIELD_DEFENSE_MULT (default 0.5; tunable).

Stamina:
- Displayed as a bar with NO numeric readout.
- Consumed by: sprint (Shift), dodge/i-frame, and certain movement skills (e.g., dashes).
- Regen rules:
  - Regen delay: starts 2.0s after last stamina consumption.
  - Regen rate: full refill in 10.0s (tunable).
  - Food/buffs may increase max stamina and/or improve regen.

Skills:
- Skills are cooldown-driven.
- Weapon weight/handling can influence cooldown feel and swing/projectile velocity  (heavy slower, light faster) (see Crafting).

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
7) COMBAT STATE, OUT-OF-COMBAT, AND DISPLACEMENT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Return Scroll eligibility depends on combat state.

Hostile categories (combat-state relevance):
- Hostile: mobs/bosses/traps caused by mobs.
- Self-inflicted: e.g., fall damage (treated as non-hostile for combat-state unless specified).
- Friendly: displacement from allies / objects / interactions.
- Neutral: environmental hazards/factors with no owner (lava, glue, ice, swimming, etc.).

Combat-state timestamps:
- t_last_hostile_damage (default “calm” gate: 40.0s; tunable)
- t_last_combat_action (default “calm” gate: 15.0s; tunable)
- Optional: t_last_forced_displacement (if needed for specific hazards)

Combat actions:
- Any action tagged “in_combat_when_used” updates t_last_combat_action.
- Movement, camera, and jump do NOT count as combat actions by default.
- Tags control edge cases (consumables, utility skills, etc.).

Displacement:
- Only intentional combat actions, hostile damage, and forced displacement EVENTS can affect combat state.
- Continuous passive motion (platform carrying you) does not repeatedly retrigger combat state.

8) DAMAGE, CRIT, AND DOTS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Base damage model (PvE):
Core values  and how they come 

(∑ Attack Mod) * (3+ 0.03(∑ Power)) + ( Base Attack) = Attack
(Attack) * (Skill base) * (∑ %Damage Mod) = Raw Damage

(∑ Defense Mod) * (3+(∑ Endurance/100)) + ( Base Defense) = Raw Defense
(Raw Defense) * (1- ∑%Damage reduction Modifier) = True Defense

(Raw Damage) / (Target True Defense) = True Damage

Total Attack Damage Modifier = Weapons + Rings + Necklace + Gloves + Belt + Bracelet

Total Defense Modifier = Helmet + Chest + Pants + Boots + Earrings + Belt + Shield  type weapons 

Endurance = gear sub-stat + food + buffs 

Power = Gear sub-stat + food + buffs

Basic Attack =0.2 * (rank)
Basic Defense =0.15 * (rank)
Base HP = 0.1 * (rank)
rank value :

S= 1200
A= 800
B= 600
C= 350
D= 200
E= 120
F= 50


TOTAL HP = (Base HP) * (1 + max HP stats) * (1+ 0.4 * max stamina )


(Base ASPD) * (1 + ∑ %ASPD bonus) = True ASPD

where ASPD = Attack Speed , that includes animation speed and cast speed Note: dose not apply to items like scrolls  only skills and animations unless speccified  otherwise 

([Base Movement Speed] * [1+ ∑%Movement Speed bonus] ) + (∑#Movement Speed bonus) = True Movement Speed ≤ 240.

Attack Damage x Skill Damage = Total Damage to target 

Damage Taken Calculation = (Base Damage - Defense Reduction) * (1 + Damage Modifiers - Damage Reduction Modifiers

Heal is calculated by

(Skill Base) * (1+ [([∑ #Heal Bonus] + [1+ ∑ %Heal Bonus])/1000]) * (1+∑ %received heal bonus on target) = True Heal

CRIT_BONUS = CRIT_MULT - 1
HEAL_CRIT_SCALAR = 0.75   (tunable)
HEAL_TRUE_CRIT = HEAL_TRUE * (1 + HEAL_CRIT_SCALAR * CRIT_BONUS)



Crit (core scaling mechanic):
- Crit Chance: 0–100% (100% = always crit).
- Crit Damage: base is +100% (2x). Can scale very high (e.g., 10x+) via gear/food/buffs.
- Crit applies to number-based outputs from SKILLS, not items.

What can crit:
- Direct heals (single hit, not HoT).
- Stationary periodic healing zones (crit roll/bonus fixed at cast time).
- Direct damage hits (multi-hit attacks roll per hit).
- Magic hits and repeated ground-effect ticks, as long as they are skill-origin and not a persistent DoT attached to the target.

What cannot crit:
- DoTs (burn/poison/etc.).
- Item damage.
- Environmental damage (lava/fire) unless explicitly specified.

DoTs:
- DoTs are “fluff on top”: fixed damage over a few seconds; duration can be extended/reset.
- DoTs do NOT crit.
- For core calculations, treat DoTs as True Damage.
- Rare special materials MAY allow % target max HP DoT (optional later).


────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
9) THREAT / AGGRO
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Threat exists and enemies maintain a threat table / target list.

Threat sources:
- Damage generates threat.
- Healing generates threat equivalent to healing output (healers must throttle; no infinite spam).
- Resurrection generates a large threat spike:
  - Default: 250% “base damage equivalent” threat (tunable).

Aggro/Tank buff:
- Aggro is not a “taunt button.” It is a threat multiplier that mimics extra damage without reducing HP.
- Current baseline: +150% virtual threat on top of real damage.
  - If you deal D damage, threat counts as 2.5 * D while buffed.
- DPS can pull aggro if played aggressively; aggro swaps are possible via coordinated throttling/burst.

Design intent:
- Tanking is an orchestrated fight for control, not a binary switch.
- Threat buff numbers are a single tuning lever to match expected DPS outputs.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
10) CC SYSTEM (WINDOWS + COOP COMBOS)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
CC is a binary on/off rule:
- 100% lands when allowed.
- 0% lands when not allowed.

CC types:
- Stun
- Knockdown
- Snare (airborne stun variant)
- Slow (airborne knockdown variant)

Base CC duration:
- 3.0 seconds (tunable; can be modified by gear/stats later)

Normal mobs:
- CC works freely (cooldowns and skillshots are the limiting factor).
- Multi-user chains can perma-stun normal mobs if executed well.

Elites:
- Stun: can land up to 3 times total; then immune until reset/death.
- Knockdown: immune (damage still applies).
- Slow: reduced effectiveness compared to normals.

Bosses:
- CC only works inside explicit CC windows (mechanic phases).
- Boss CC success requires multiple coordinated CC applications:
  - e.g., 3 to 7 successful CC hits (tunable by floor difficulty and attack type).
- Wrong CC type during the window resets progress.
- Non-CC damage does NOT affect CC progress (so DPS can keep DPSing as long as they avoid the wrong CC inputs).

Airborne CC variants:
- Using a “stun skill” while airborne results in Snare (horizontal lock).
- Using a “knockdown skill” while airborne results in Slow (daze).
- UI support: skill icons can swap while airborne to communicate the alternate effect.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
11) DOWNED / DEATH / RECOVERY (RUN RISK SYSTEM)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Downed:
- HP hits zero.
- Visual grayscale; camera look allowed; no movement/skills.
- UI:
  - “Give Up” button (surrender -> return to town)
  - “Request Res” ping button (signals you are present and want resurrection)

Bleedout / auto-return:
- After 15 minutes downed, auto return to town.
- Auto-return counts as full death (full durability penalty).

Durability:
- No durability decay unless death-related.
- Downed: durability loss ~1.5% on equipped gear (tunable).
- Full death/return (give up or auto-return): additional ~1.5% (total ~3%).
- Accessories do not break.
- At 0 durability:
  - item grants no stats (grayscale)
  - item visually removed from character
  - weapons auto-unequip; player uses a basic “unnamed” kit (TBD)

Scrolls (core risk tools):
A) Return Scroll (Extraction to town)
- Casting requires Return eligibility rules (Section 12).
- Cast time: 15 seconds. Interrupted by:
  - damage
  - hard CC
  - any movement input
  - any ability use
- On success: creates 15m extraction zone for 60 seconds.
- Entering zone triggers “Return?” prompt.
- Prompt persists up to 3 minutes (AFK tolerance).
- Dead players can accept; on return, all spawn alive in town.

B) Resurrection Scroll (Revive on location)
- Not restricted by out-of-combat status; can be used under pressure.
- 4m AOE revive at current coordinates.
- Accept window: 30 seconds.
- Cooldown starts on BUTTON PRESS (even if cast fails).
- Base cooldown: 10 minutes.
- House of Prayer reduces cooldown (target: -75% -> ~2m30s).

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
12) RETURN SCROLL ELIGIBILITY (LOCKED VALUES)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
A player may START casting Return Scroll only if ALL are true:
1) Primary Target Rule:
   - The player is NOT the current “#1 active target” of ANY enemy.
2) Hostile Damage Calm:
   - now - t_last_hostile_damage >= 40.0 seconds (tunable)
3) Combat Action Calm:
   - now - t_last_combat_action >= 15.0 seconds (tunable)

Notes:
- Movement/jump do not count as combat actions by default.
- Actions are controlled via “in_combat_when_used” tagging.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
13) ENEMY CHASE / RESET (NO “MEMORY” ON RESET)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Chase is designed to prevent “dragging bosses across the floor” and to avoid “feels like a bug” aggro memory.

- Enemies chase participants normally while within chase constraints.
- If chase/pathing/resource constraints are exceeded, the enemy performs a CLEAN RESET:
  - returns to zone
  - clears aggro/target list (no remembered participants)
  - HP/behavior reset rules TBD per enemy type (standard reset baseline)

Budgets (design intent):
- Normal mobs: long chase budget
- Elites: longer chase budget
- Bosses: short chase budget

14) QUESTS, CONTRACTS, & SOCIAL SYSTEMS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Quest board (NPC):
- Quest ranks: F → E → D → C → B → A → S
- Rank unlocks based on thresholds of completed quests.
- Higher ranks grant better rewards/odds (tunable).

Player contracts (matchmaking disguised as quests):
- Players can post:
  - “Help me kill X boss / clear objective”
  - “Escort/Guide me (map exploration + protection)”
  - “Bring X items/materials”
  - “Commission craft” (design/service fee models)
- Contracts can be used as party board / queue system for repeating farms.

Escrow:
- Contract rewards are deposited up-front into system escrow (items/currency).
- Completion verification pays automatically.
- If expired/cancelled, escrow returns minus fee (TBD).
- Purpose: reduce scams; logs remain available for disputes.

Reporting & moderation:
- Report player via chat or character card.
- Repeat offenders: warning → timed suspension on next login session (time starts when they attempt to play).

Inspection:
- Players can inspect gear via character card; privacy toggle exists.

Mail:
- Letter system (30-day expiry) for messages + item sending.
- “Claim all” option planned.

16) CRAFTING & THE FORGE (CORE FEATURE)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Philosophy:
- Power isn’t found; it’s made.
- Weapons/armor are not stat sticks; they change gameplay behavior.
- Constraints are deliberate; creativity comes from working within boundaries.

The Forge (personal instanced workspace):
- Accessed via hub portal/door.
- Private to the player (optional: allow small group visitors for testing/showcase).
- Contains:
  0. Processing Station
  1. Mats Storage (filtered search)
  2. Weapon Crafting Station
  3. Armor Crafting Station
  4. Accessory Crafting Station
  5. Skill Crafting Station
  6. Testing Dummies
  7. Semi-finished Storage
  8. Naming Station (finalize & export item)

Processing Station:
- Converts raw drops into “material cubes” (mats).
- Once processed, mats are UNTRADEABLE until they become a final item.
- Processing options include:
  - extract “as is”
  - extract color separately (for dye systems)
  - create raw stat mats (gray) that can be painted later
- Higher quality mats have more gloss/metallic aesthetic (visual only).
- Recycling: dismantle crafted items back into components at a loss.
- Blueprint extraction: extract blueprint + skills + recipe/material list (used for replication/upgrading).

Mats Storage:
- Search/filter by:
  - color
  - bonus effects
  - gear quality (gray/green/blue/purple/orange)

Weapon Crafting Station (visual build):
- Grid volume: 160 x 80 x 40 (stacked plates)
- Maximum fill: 35% of grid volume (constraint).
- Symmetry tools:
  - mirror quarters
  - mirror halves (top/bottom, left/right)
- Bulk tools & brushes:
  - ghost blocks + bucket fill
  - spherical brush (multi-layer painting)
  - single-face paint tool
  - select-all recolor
  - erase tools (single/multi, cut-through toggle)
  - revert up to 30 steps
  - edge tools (round bevel / 45-degree bevel)

Armor/Accessory crafting:
- Crafted around mannequin constraints (no placement inside body volume).
- Same toolset; grid adapted per component.

Skill Crafting Station (behavior design):
- Allocate elements to buttons/skills based on mats in the weapon.
- Choose grip points (1H/2H), balance points, swing arcs, trajectory behavior.
- Support advanced behavior templates (saved to the weapon template).
- Archetype selection is made here by the player (backend classification; not shown as “weapon types” to players).

Naming Station:
- Final step required before item can leave Forge, be equipped, or traded.
- Crafter must finalize tooltips/descriptions before naming (to prevent “blank” items).

Crafting budgets:
- Each block carries values inherited from its drop.
- Valid shapes must include a proper handle/grip system.
- Weight/balance influences:
  - damage potential
  - attack speed/feel
  - cooldown feel
  - movement speed modifiers (sprint overrides for consistency)

Shields:
- Shields are crafted similarly to weapons but with fixed anchor constraints.
- Shields have their own restricted behavior patterns and higher block/parry focus.
- Shields can be main-hand or offhand; dual shields are possible (not optimal).

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
17) TRADING, PROVENANCE, AND BLUEPRINT OWNERSHIP
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Trading tiers:
- Raw drops: tradeable.
- Processed mats (“material cubes”): untradeable until finalized into an item.
- Final items: tradeable (rules may apply).

Provenance:
- Items are traceable: “designed by @playername” remains attached.
- Ownership history can be recorded (handoffs count, previous owners list).

Trade limits (crafted gear):
- Crafted gear can have a limited number of trades before binding (exact count TBD).
- Creator’s first sale does not count against trade limit (TBD as rule).

Blueprint extraction & trademark design:
- Any finished item can be sacrificed to extract a blueprint.
- Extracted blueprint retains the original “designed by @playername” tag.
- Blueprint skills cannot be modified; only material quality can be upgraded by following the stored recipe/material list.
- Intent: creators sell “designs,” buyers invest their rare mats to craft high-quality versions.

18) HOUSES (FACTIONS / OATHS)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Purpose:
- Opt-in identity layer (not locked at character creation).
- Adds passive bonuses + dedicated house skill hotbar (limited set; exact skills TBD).

Current houses (draft):

- House of Might: physical single-target bias (stats + effects).
- House of Wisdom: magic/elemental bias + perception (traps/markers).
- House of Fortification: defense/stamina/anti-stagger + threat management.
