Alright — here’s the **Narrator’s Cut**.

Not a pitch.
Not a feature list.
Not a design doc.

This is:

> *What it feels like to step into this world.*

Based on everything you've built so far.

---

## The Story of the World — As a Player Would Tell It

You don’t begin as a hero.

You begin as someone who **should have been**.

Something happened before the world even had a chance to know you.

A celestial rock fell from the sky.

You survived — barely.

But whatever you were… is gone.

Not your body.

Not your presence.

Your *memory*.

---

You wake up in a city you’ve never seen — surrounded by people who found you, carried you, and decided you were worth saving.

A doctor asks questions.

Simple ones.

Do you remember your people?

Do you remember your craft?

Do you remember how you fought?

You answer as best you can.

And the world begins to shape itself around those answers.

You don’t “pick a class.”

You remember a life.

Or think you do.

And from that moment forward, your path begins.

---

## The City

Everyone is here.

Not because the game told them to be.

Because this is where stories begin.

You hear names.

Some spoken with admiration.

Some with resentment.

You see people wearing titles you don’t yet understand.

You watch guilds preparing for something you haven’t yet learned about.

You overhear:

> “They cleared 37 last season.”
> “She forged the blade that made it possible.”
> “They found a route nobody thought existed.”

The city isn’t a waiting room.

It’s a living memory.

---

## The Floors

When people leave the city, they don’t go to missions.

They go to **floors**.

Vast layered spaces that feel more like landscapes than dungeons.

You don’t rush through them.

You *travel* through them.

Sometimes it takes an hour just to reach the thing you came for.

Sometimes the journey becomes more important than the destination.

You begin to realize:

The floors aren’t puzzles.

They’re problems.

And every group solves them differently.

---

## The Work

Nothing of value falls neatly into your hands.

You don’t chase loot.

You chase possibility.

A scale from something that once ruled the depths.

A thread spun by creatures most people ignore.

A fragment of bone that hums when held.

Individually — they are curiosities.

Together — they become intention.

You bring them back.

To someone who knows what to do with them.

Or maybe you become that someone.

---

## The Creation

Here, power isn’t found.

It’s made.

Not just by recipe.

By decision.

A weapon matters not because it exists…

…but because of what went into it.

Who gathered those parts.

Who understood their nature.

Who shaped them into purpose.

Some names become known not for what they fought —

but for what they made possible.

---

## The Knowledge

Over time, you start to see things others miss.

Routes that weren’t obvious.

Patterns in how dangers emerge.

Moments where preparation matters more than strength.

You realize that the strongest people here aren’t lucky.

They’re aware.

They know.

And that knowledge becomes their edge.

---

## The Seasons

Just when the world starts to feel understood —

it changes.

Not by erasing what you’ve done.

But by asking:

> Can you do it again?

With new paths.

New unknowns.

New races to the top.

Old veterans return.

Newcomers arrive.

Everyone remembers more than their character ever could.

And the race begins again.

---

## The Gatherings

Sometimes — the entire world moves at once.

A threat emerges.

Not for a team.

Not for a guild.

For everyone.

You join.

Alongside strangers.

Some you’ll never see again.

Some you’ll work with for years.

Victory doesn’t belong to the strongest swing.

It belongs to the best prepared.

---

## The Rivalries

Not all challenges live in the depths.

Some exist between people.

A contested space.

A dangerous one.

Where risk and reward dance together.

Where alliances are formed — or broken.

Where the winner isn’t always the strongest faction…

…but the one that understands timing.

Coordination.

Protection.

And when to let pride fall for a greater gain.

---

## The Legacy

Names start to matter.

Not because of levels.

Because of moments.

First clears.

Impossible saves.

Brilliant creations.

Seasonal triumphs.

People remember:

> “They were there.”
> “They figured it out.”
> “They made it happen.”

---

## What You Eventually Understand

This world isn’t about becoming the best.

It’s about becoming known.

Not through power.

Through understanding.

Through cooperation.

Through intention.

And through the quiet moments when you show someone:

> “Come here… I want to show you something I found.”

---

## The Truth

You never recover your memory.

But it doesn’t matter.

Because over time —

you become someone new.

And this time…

The world remembers you.
