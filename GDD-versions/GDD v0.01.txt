1) HIGH CONCEPT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
A hub-based, PvE-first, instanced “99-floor” dungeon expedition game with:
- 3D skillshot combat (including melee), dodging, blocking, CC windows.
- No hard classes: your equipment + crafted weapon shape determines playstyle.
- Deep crafting via block-based design: shape + materials create unique weapons/armor.
- Floors rotate in difficulty each season: the named floor stays the same layout, but its floor number (difficulty & drop quality) changes.
- Social hub + contracts (player-driven “quests”) to form parties and trade.

Player fantasy:
- “I craft a weapon nobody else has, learn the place, and push higher difficulty versions of the same floor.”
- “People matter, knowledge matters, creation matters.”

Core pillars:
1) People matter (party coordination, contracts, reputation, crafting identity)
2) Knowledge matters (map discovery, learning enemy patterns, floor mastery)
3) Creation matters (crafted gear defines gameplay; provenance; templates)
4) Seasons create memory (rotating difficulty shifts the focus without wiping knowledge)

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
2) CORE LOOP
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Gather → Craft → Attempt Floor → Extract/Return → Refine Build → Climb Difficulty

- Go to a floor (instance)
- Explore (personal fog-of-war), gather materials, fight mobs/bosses
- Craft or commission gear, adjust build
- Retry higher difficulty versions of known layouts for better material quality
- Chase clears, titles, and social prestige

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
3) WORLD STRUCTURE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Hub/Town:
- Minimal prototype hub initially: 4x4 platform + station placeholders
- Stations (planned): Crafting station, Storage chest, Vendor, Cooking station, Repair NPC
- Social: chat, mail, contracts board, party forming, inspection

Floors (99):
- Each floor is a NAMED layout (e.g., “Grace’s Garden”) with a stable map/layout identity.
- Seasonal rotation remaps names to floor numbers (difficulty & drop mod).
- Layout stays constant; difficulty and drop quality shift with floor number.
- At higher difficulty placements MAY add extra enemies/events (optional later).

Instance model:
- Players are in one instance at a time (hub OR a floor instance).
- Client only loads current scene; server can run many instances concurrently (future).

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
4) EXPLORATION & MAP DISCOVERY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Fog-of-war:
- Map reveal is PERSONAL and permanent once discovered for a named floor.
- Party members are always visible on the map as dot + name tag (even in fog).
- Teleporting into unknown reveals a local “island” bubble around landing point.

Party-size reveal bonus (low impact):
- Reveal radius is centered on YOUR character.
- If party members are physically within your reveal radius, you gain a small bonus (cap ~+20%).
- Intended to mildly reward coordinated exploration; not balance-critical.

Design intent:
- Easy difficulty versions emphasize discovery/navigation.
- Hard difficulty versions emphasize combat mastery without being “lost in danger.”

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
5) COMBAT OVERVIEW
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Combat style:
- 3D OTS camera, zoom option.
- All attacks are skillshots (including melee).
- Hit only counts if target is in attack volume; i-frames exist only on dedicated skills.

No classes:
- Equipment defines role and gameplay.
- Weapons determine how fixed “skill identity slots” behave.

Blocking:
- Blocking mitigates damage; shields provide a physical barrier that can protect allies behind.
- Blocking effectiveness scales with stats/weapon/shield profile.

Aggro/threat:
- Threat exists and enemies track a target list for each pull.
- No “drop aggro” feature. If you were engaged in the pull, you remain a valid target until:
  A) mob dies, OR
  B) you leave the floor via death/down/return, OR
  C) you get extracted.

Tank support:
- Aggro buff exists (e.g., +40% threat generation, tunable).
- Purpose: allow anyone to tank via preparation, not weapon restriction.

CC system (cooperative + mechanic-driven):
- CC requires multiple applications of the same CC type within a timed window.
- Many enemies have CC windows; if not in window, CC does not stick.
- Wrong CC type can fail a mechanic (enemy response, retaliation, cleanse, etc.).

Enemy chase model (no infinite dragging bosses):
- Pursue: enemy actively chases if reachable/reasonable.
- Hunt: if pursuit exceeds budget or pathing becomes unreliable, enemy returns to its area BUT keeps targets flagged; re-engages instantly if flagged targets re-enter detection range.
Budgets:
- Normal mobs: long pursue budget
- Elites: longer pursue budget
- Bosses: short pursue budget (prevents “drag boss across floor”)

6) DOWNED / DEATH / RECOVERY (RUN RISK SYSTEM)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
State definitions:
- Downed = HP hits zero.
  - Visual grayscale; camera look allowed; no movement/skills.
  - UI: “Give Up” button (return to town).
  - UI: “Request Res” ping button (signals you are present and want resurrection).
- Auto-return: if downed for 15 minutes, auto return to town (instance cleanup).

Durability:
- No durability decay unless death-related.
- Downed: durability loss ~1.5% on equipped gear (tunable).
- Full death/return: double cost (~3%).
- Accessories do not break.
- At 0 durability:
  - item grants no stats (grayscale)
  - item visually removed from character
  - weapons auto-unequip; player uses a basic “unnamed” kit (TBD).

Scrolls (core risk tools):
A) Return Scroll (Extraction to town)
- Casting REQUIRES combat status condition (see below).
- Cast time: 15 seconds. Interrupted by:
  - damage
  - hard CC
  - ANY movement input
  - ANY ability use
- On success: creates 15m extraction zone for 60 seconds.
- Entering zone triggers “Return?” prompt.
- Prompt persists for up to 3 minutes (AFK tolerance).
- Dead players can accept; on return, all spawn alive in town.

B) Resurrection Scroll (Revive on location)
- Not restricted by combat status (can be used under pressure).
- Works similarly to Return scroll but:
  - radius ~4m (tunable)
  - revive occurs at current coordinates
- Accept window: TBD (e.g., 30 seconds)
- Cooldown: 10 min base; House of Prayer reduces cooldown (target ~2m30s).
- Cooldown starts on BUTTON PRESS (even if cast fails).

Return Scroll Combat Status Rule (v0.02):
- A player may start casting Return Scroll only if:
  1) They are NOT the current “#1 active target” of any enemy (primary target rule), AND
  2) They have not taken damage for X seconds, AND
  3) They have not dealt damage/applied hostile effects for Y seconds.
- Rationale: solo cannot freely extract while enemy is alive; groups can coordinate exits by having someone else be the primary target.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
7) SKILLS & ABILITY SYSTEM
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Fixed skill identity slots:
- Slot roles are constant (e.g., Primary / Secondary / Mobility / Defense / Utility…).
- Weapon profile changes what each slot does (range, shape, damage flavor, movement, etc.).
- Goal: avoid hardcoding classes and allow weapon-driven gameplay.

Buttons/UI:
- Target: at least 24 bindable combat buttons + dedicated consumable slots (~12) + additional house skill hotbar.
- UI is modular/customizable; players can move blocks and save presets.

I-frames:
- Only on dedicated skills (usually mobility/defense).
- I-frame windows are explicit and limited.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
8) CRAFTING & GEAR (CORE FEATURE)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Philosophy:
- Weapons/armor are not stat sticks. They change how you play.
- Crafting requires thought/intent; outcomes are coherent and predictable (not pure randomness).

Block-based crafting:
- Items are designed using a grid of square blocks.
- Shape determines archetype/function; materials determine stats and bonuses.
- Number of blocks influences weight/nimbleness (speed vs power tradeoffs).

Weapon profiles (derived from shape + materials):
- Mass, Reach, Balance, Damage Flavor (cut/blunt/pierce), Tags (rope/pole/ranged/magic), Flex segments, etc.
- These profiles feed into skill slot templates to generate attack patterns.

Armor/accessories:
- Similar block-based logic. Accessories do not break.

Durability & repair:
- Repair restores durability; items not destroyed at 0 durability.
- Repair uses currency (see Economy) and/or services (TBD).

Visual style:
- Voxel/micro-block “pixel-dense” style to keep crafted forms readable and consistent.
- Crafting UI may use finer resolution; gameplay may use simplified silhouettes/LOD to preserve readability.

Templates:
- Players can save design templates.
- Armor may be designed in stacked 2D layers to form 3D items (TBD).
- Mannequin preview before committing materials (planned).

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
9) LOOT, TIERS, & PROGRESSION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Loot tiers:
- Gray → Green → Blue → Purple → Orange
- Higher floor numbers increase chances for higher-tier materials.
- Same material name can exist in different tiers (same function, stronger stats).

Harvesting:
- No tool gating. Players can loot/harvest without special tools.
- (Optional later) tools give speed/bonus yield but never block harvesting.

Crafting proficiency:
- Crafting XP gained by crafting; higher-tier materials grant more crafting XP.
- Apprenticeship/quest unlocks crafting profession (planned).

Progression:
- Power progression comes mainly from crafted gear quality + mastery + learning patterns.
- Map knowledge persists for named floors (progression via knowledge).

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
10) QUESTS, CONTRACTS, & SOCIAL SYSTEMS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Quest board:
- Quest ranks: F → E → D → C → B → A → S
- Rank unlocks based on thresholds of completed quests.
- Higher ranks give better rewards/odds (tunable).

Player contracts (bounties as matchmaking + economy):
- Players can post requests:
  - “Help me kill X boss / clear floor objective”
  - “Escort me / guide exploration”
  - “Bring me X materials / crafted item order”
- Contracts are tokenized with restrictions to prevent spam/abuse.

Escrow (anti-scam):
- Contract rewards are deposited up-front into system escrow (materials/gear/currency).
- On verified completion, escrow pays automatically.
- If expired/cancelled, escrow returns minus fee (TBD).

Reporting:
- Player report system via chat/player card.
- Repeat offenders: automated warning → timed suspension on next login session.
- Logs are authoritative for dispute review.

Inspection:
- Players can inspect gear via character card; privacy toggle available.

Mail:
- Letter system (30-day expiry) for messages + item sending.
- “Claim all” feature (planned).

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
11) ECONOMY & CURRENCY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Primary trading:
- Item-for-item barter is supported and encouraged culturally.

Currency:
- One currency with denominations: Copper / Silver / Gold (conversion like change).
- Currency used primarily for NPC services and friction systems:
  - repairs
  - scroll purchases
  - contract posting fees
  - vendor basics
Goal: money exists but isn’t the soul of the economy.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
12) HOUSES (FACTIONS / OATHS)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Purpose:
- Opt-in identity layer and build flavor; NOT locked at character creation.
- Adds passive bonuses + a small set of dedicated “house skills” on its own hotbar.

Current houses (draft):
- House of Prayer: revive/support/healing utility; reduces resurrection scroll cooldown.
- House of Might: physical single-target/pierce bias (stats + effects).
- House of Wisdom: magic/elemental bias + perception (traps/markers).
- House of Fortification: defense/stamina/anti-stagger + threat management.

Notes:
- House effects are intentionally unrefined until combat system is finalized.
- Must be balanced to avoid mandatory roles.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
15) GLOSSARY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Named Floor: A unique layout/theme that stays constant (e.g., “Grace’s Garden”).
Floor Number: Current difficulty/drop modifier assigned to a Named Floor this season.
Downed: HP=0 state; immobilized; can request res or give up.
Return Scroll: AOE extraction to town; cast requires not being #1 target + calm window.
Resurrection Scroll: AOE revive at location; can be cast in combat; cooldown starts on press.
Escrow: System holds contract reward and pays on completion to prevent scams.
House/Oath: Opt-in faction layer; passive + limited skills; not locked at character creation.
